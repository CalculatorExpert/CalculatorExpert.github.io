Frequently Asked Questions

What is the 4% rule?

It's a rule of thumb suggesting you can safely withdraw 4% of your portfolio in the first year of retirement and adjust for inflation thereafter.

When should I start saving for retirement?

The earlier the better! Thanks to compound interest, money invested in your 20s grows significantly more than money invested in your 40s.

How much do I need to retire?

A common goal is to have 25 times your annual expenses saved to follow the 4% withdrawal rule.
